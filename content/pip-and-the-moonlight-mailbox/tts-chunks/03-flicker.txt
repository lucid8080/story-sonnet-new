The second glowing dot led toward the old stump at the edge of the garden, where the grasses grew tall and the air buzzed faintly.

That was where Pip found Flicker.

Flicker was a firefly, but he was not flashing.
He sat on a mushroom looking worried.

Hello, Pip said gently. Are you Flicker?

Maybe, said the firefly in a tiny voice. I used to be. But tonight my glow feels all wobbly. What if I blink at the wrong time? What if everyone else twinkles perfectly and I go plink instead of blink?

Pip sat down on the moss nearby.
That sounds hard, they said.

Flicker nodded sadly.
It is hard. I practiced three times and worried six times.

That is a lot of worrying, Pip agreed. Good thing I brought moon mail.

Flicker opened the gold-speckled envelope.
Inside, the note read:

Dear Flicker,
The night does not need perfect light.
It needs your light.
Small glows count.
Wobbly glows count.
Brave little blinks count too.

Flicker read it twice.
Then a tiny dot of golden light appeared at the tip of his tail.

Blink.
Then another.
Blink-blink.

Soon he was glowing like a warm star tucked into a teacup.

It worked! cried Flicker.

He zipped in a happy loop around Pip’s head, then hovered still.

Take this, he said.
He shook loose a pinch of glow dust into Pip’s palm. It sparkled like cheerful sugar.
If the last delivery gets gloomy, toss the dust into the air. It reminds things how to shine.

Pip thanked him and checked the map.
Only one glowing dot remained.
