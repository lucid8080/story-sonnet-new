Pip and the Moonlight Mailbox.
Episode one: The Letter for the Bravest Good Helper.
For ages four to six.

Pip lived in a little yellow house with a creaky garden gate, a patch of mint by the steps, and the softest nighttime breeze in the whole neighborhood.

Every evening, Pip brushed their teeth, put on moon-and-star pajamas, and peeked outside just in case something interesting had decided to happen.

Most nights, the garden looked ordinary.
A sleepy fence.
A round birdbath.
A row of daisies nodding like they were almost asleep.

But on the brightest moon nights, something magical appeared at the end of the garden path.

A tiny blue mailbox.

It was never there in the daytime.
Only at night.
Only in moonlight.
And only when the world felt quiet enough to hear a secret.

Pip had seen it twice before, glowing softly like it had swallowed a little star. But tonight, the mailbox had its tiny silver door popped open.

Inside was a letter.

On the front, written in swirly silver ink, were the words:
To the Bravest Good Helper Nearby.

Pip looked over one shoulder.
Then the other.
The garden was empty.

Bravest good helper? Pip whispered.
That must be... me?

The letter gave a polite little shimmer, as if to say,
Yes. Obviously.

Pip opened it carefully.

Inside was a moon-white note.

Dear Pip,
Three important letters must be delivered before sunrise.
One to a sleepy hedgehog.
One to a nervous firefly.
One to a little cloud that has forgotten how to rain gently.
Please hurry. The moon is waiting.
