At the bottom was a silver map no bigger than Pip’s hand.
It sparkled with three glowing dots.

Pip took a deep breath.
This was definitely bigger than finding a lost sock.
This was maybe even bigger than feeding the neighbor’s goldfish.

But the night was soft.
The letter felt warm.
And somebody needed help.

So Pip nodded.
All right, they said. I can do brave. I think. Probably. Tiny brave still counts.

The mailbox gave a pleased little ding.

Inside were three envelopes.
One smelled faintly like blackberries.
One blinked with tiny gold specks.
One was cool and misty, like the air right before rain.

Pip tucked the letters into a satchel, slipped on rain boots, and stepped through the moonlit gate.

The first glowing dot on the map led to Bramble Hollow, where hedgehogs liked to nap under blackberry bushes.

The path wiggled past the birdbath, behind the daisies, and through a patch of tall grass that whispered against Pip’s boots.

Soon Pip heard a sound.
Snrrrff.
Snrrrff.
Snuffle-snore.

There, curled into a prickly little comma under the blackberry leaves, was Hazel the hedgehog. She was wearing a lavender sleep cap with one floppy pom-pom.

Hazel? Pip whispered.

Hazel did not move.

Pip tried again.
Hazel? I have moon mail.

One shiny black nose twitched.
One eye blinked open.

Moon mail? Hazel mumbled. At this hour? It had better be cozy.

Pip handed her the blackberry-scented envelope.
Hazel opened it and read:

Dear Hazel,
Thank you for keeping the bramble paths neat and beetle-safe.
Tonight the moon awards you one extra excellent nap.

Hazel put a paw over her heart.
Why, she said, that is cozy.

Then she reached under a leaf and pulled out a tiny lantern made from a dew drop and a button.

For your journey, Hazel said. The next path is dim.

Pip clipped the lantern to the satchel.
Its soft light bobbed like a friendly firefly.
