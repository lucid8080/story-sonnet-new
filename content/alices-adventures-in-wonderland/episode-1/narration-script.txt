🎧 Alice and the First Curious Step




Narrator (warm, gentle):

It was a quiet, golden afternoon…

The kind of afternoon where even the clouds
seemed a little sleepy.

Alice sat beneath a tall tree,
her book resting open in her lap.

She turned a page…

…then another…

…and then—

she sighed.

Alice (softly):
“Why do stories never change?”

She looked out across the grass.

“I wish something… unexpected would happen.”

And just then—

TAP. TAP. TAP.

A sound.

Not loud.

Not fast.

But very… precise.

Alice sat up.

“Hello?”

From behind the tree—

a small white rabbit stepped into view.

Wearing a tiny coat.

Holding a pocket watch.

White Rabbit (muttering kindly):
“Not late… not early… just not quite right…”

Alice’s eyes widened.

“…Did that rabbit just speak?”

The Rabbit glanced up—

and for a moment…

he smiled.

Then—

WHOOSH!

He darted away.

Alice (excited):
“Wait! Come back!”

🎶 (Music becomes playful, curious)

Alice jumped to her feet and followed—

across the grass,
past the tree,
and straight toward a small, round hole at the base of a hill.

The Rabbit slipped inside.

Alice paused.

She leaned closer.

Inside the hole—

there was no darkness.

Only a soft, glowing swirl…

like the inside of a dream.

Alice (whispering):
“…Well…”

She took a breath.

“…that’s certainly unexpected.”

And with one small, brave step—

she climbed in.

🎶 (Music shifts—floating, magical)

Down she went…

but not like falling.

No—

this was more like drifting.

Teacups floated past her.

A clock ticked… backwards.

A deck of cards fluttered by like butterflies.

Alice (laughing softly):
“Oh! Oh, this is wonderful!”

As she floated—

she noticed something strange.

The deeper she went…

the brighter everything felt.

Not just brighter to see—

but brighter to feel.

Curious.

Exciting.

A little wobbly.

With a gentle poof—

Alice landed.

She stood up slowly.

Brushed off her dress.

And looked around.

Trees with curling branches.

Mushrooms with glowing spots.

Paths that twisted… and turned… and sometimes… disappeared.

Alice (in awe):
“…Where am I?”

A voice answered—

but not from anywhere she could see.

Cheshire Cat (soft, playful):
“Somewhere that listens.”

Alice turned.

“…Hello?”

High above—

two glowing eyes appeared.

Then a smile.

A wide, floating grin.

Cheshire Cat:
“You were looking for something unexpected.”

Alice nodded.

“…I was.”

Cheshire Cat:
“Then you stepped exactly where you needed to.”

Alice looked down at her feet.

The ground shimmered slightly.

As if it had been waiting.

Alice:
“Is this… Wonderland?”

The Cat’s grin grew wider.

Cheshire Cat:
“Only if you wonder.”

Alice took a slow step forward.

Then another.

And with each step—

the world seemed to respond.

A flower leaned closer.

A path appeared.

A breeze carried laughter.

Alice (softly, smiling):
“I think… I like it here.”

In the distance—

the White Rabbit peeked out from behind a tree.

Watching.

Making sure she had arrived safely.

Alice looked ahead—

at all the strange and wonderful things waiting for her.

She didn’t know what would happen next.

She didn’t know what she might find.

But she did know one thing.

She had taken her first curious step.

And Wonderland…

had opened.

Narrator (soft, closing):

And that…

was how Alice found her way
into Wonderland.
