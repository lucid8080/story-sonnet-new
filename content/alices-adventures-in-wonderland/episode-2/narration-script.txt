🎧 Episode 2: Alice and the Ever-Changing Size Day



Narrator (warm, familiar):

After her very first curious step into Wonderland…
Alice had learned something important.

This was a place that didn’t just look magical…

It felt magical too.

And the more she explored…

the more Wonderland seemed to respond to her.

On this particular morning,
Alice wandered down a winding path lined with glowing mushrooms.

She smiled.

“I wonder what today will be like.”

RUSH!

A blur of white zipped past her again.

White Rabbit (panicked, but gentle):
“Alice! Oh—Alice! Thank goodness I found you!”

Alice laughed.

“You always seem in a hurry.”

Rabbit (checking watch):
“Yes, but today is very important!”

He leaned closer.

“It’s Size Day.”

Alice tilted her head.

“Size Day?”

Before the Rabbit could explain—

POP!

A mushroom beside them suddenly shrank to the size of a pebble.

WHOOMP!

Another one grew taller than a house.

Alice jumped back.

“Oh! It’s doing it again!”

Rabbit (worried):
“It’s not supposed to do that randomly!”

“Everything should change in a nice, neat order!”

Alice looked around.

Nothing was neat.

Nothing was in order.

A tiny bird suddenly grew enormous—

then shrank again mid-flap.

A teacup rolled by… bigger than a wheel.

Alice (wide-eyed, but curious):
“…It’s a little wobbly, isn’t it?”

Rabbit:
“It’s completely wobbly!”

They hurried down the path until—

they reached a very familiar place.

A long, crooked table.

Teacups everywhere.

And—

Mad Hatter (dramatic sigh):
“My tea party has turned into a disaster!”

Alice stepped closer.

The Hatter was perched on a chair that was far too tiny…

his knees tucked up awkwardly.

Meanwhile—

his teapot was enormous.

Hatter:
“I pour tea…”

SPLASH!

“…and it becomes a waterfall!”

Alice couldn’t help but giggle.

Alice:
“Maybe… it’s not the teapot.”

The Hatter blinked.

“…What do you mean?”

A familiar voice drifted in.

Cheshire Cat (appearing slowly):
“Perhaps… it’s the feeling behind it.”

Alice looked up.

“Feeling again?”

The Cat’s grin stretched wide.

Cheshire Cat:
“In Wonderland… size follows feeling.”

Alice nodded slowly.

She remembered.

From her first day.

She looked at the Hatter.

“You seem a little… stressed.”

Hatter (flustered):
“Well of course I am! Everything must be perfect for Size Day!”

Alice knelt beside the table.

“What if it doesn’t have to be perfect?”

The Hatter paused.

“…Not perfect?”

Alice (gently):
“What if it just has to be… enjoyed?”

The Hatter took a breath.

A long one.

Then another.

His shoulders relaxed.

He picked up the teapot again—

this time slowly.

Carefully.

He poured.

No splash.

No wave.

Just tea.

The teapot gently shrank…

The cup grew just enough…

And everything…

fit.

Hatter (amazed):
“…Oh!”

The Rabbit checked his watch.

“It’s working! The schedule is correcting itself!”

All around Wonderland—

things began to settle.

Big became just right.

Small became just enough.

Alice stood and smiled.

Alice:
“It’s not about controlling everything…”

“…it’s about how we feel when things change.”

Above her—

the Cheshire Cat’s grin shimmered.

Cheshire Cat:
“And you’re learning quickly.”

The Hatter raised a teacup.

“To Alice! The most helpful guest in Wonderland!”

Alice laughed.

“And the most interesting tea party I’ve ever seen.”

As the sun dipped softly behind the trees—

Wonderland felt calm again.

Balanced.

Happy.

And Alice?

She felt something new.

Not just curious anymore…

…but confident.

Narrator (soft, closing):

And that…

was Alice’s first ever
Ever-Changing Size Day.
