# Cover Art Prompt — The Ube Folk of Amihan Hollow

Create a **4:5 vertical** children's-book cover illustration for an audio story series titled **"The Ube Folk of Amihan Hollow"**.

## Scene
A breezy valley of terraced ube fields at golden-hour-twilight, with magical lantern gourds glowing over rows of purple yam leaves. In the foreground, four small original purple villagers stand on a stone relay lane with a carved harvest wand, loop baskets, and woven sports gear, looking lively and ready for play. Across a bamboo footbridge in the midground, friendly rivals from Taro Village gather with drums and striped field scarves. Hidden among the terraces is the suggestion of moonlit glow roots under the soil.

## Main Character Design Notes
The Ube Folk must feel **clearly original** and **not derivative of any existing franchise**.
- small upright **pear-shaped bodies** with broad soft middles and short sturdy legs
- long flat **leaf-sail ears** that angle out like folded pennants
- long balancing feet suited for terraces and sport
- clothing made of **woven sash-belts, seed-pocket vests, rain-cape ponchos, fan-shaped leaf visors, ankle ribbons, and loop baskets**
- expressive, warm, playful faces

Feature:
- **Mina** — plum leaf visor, notebook tucked into sash, focused captain energy
- **Toto** — cheerful sturdy build, seed-pocket vest, carrying practice batons or harvest wand
- **Ilya** — silver ankle ribbons, agile runner posture
- **Bembem** — mischievous grin, gadgety ribbon spool and spoon tucked into belt

## Style
- modern whimsical children's-book illustration
- hand-painted textured digital art
- gouache / watercolor / colored-pencil feel
- visible grain and brush texture
- warm, cozy, magical palette
- decorative poster-like composition
- emotionally warm and kid-friendly

## Cultural / World Notes
- The story is inspired by the joy of **ube, the beloved purple yam**, and should gently honor that through food, farming, and celebration details.
- Include bamboo, woven textures, terrace farming, lantern gourds, river stones, and festive community energy.
- Make the sports angle feel playful and communal, not aggressive.

## Composition Notes
- Keep the Ube Folk silhouette very readable and distinct.
- Leave room for title text in the upper third.
- The valley should feel cozy, magical, and lived-in.
- No scary villains in frame.
- Friendly rivalry and harvest wonder should both be visible.

## Palette Suggestions
Ube violet, yam cream, leaf green, river teal, moon-silver, toasted coconut beige, lantern gold.

## Output
- 4:5 aspect ratio
- cover-ready composition
- no baked-in text unless requested
- suitable for Story Sonnet homepage tile and story page

If final art is not generated now, use this prompt as the source-of-truth for later cover production.
