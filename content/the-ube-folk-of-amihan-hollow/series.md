# The Ube Folk of Amihan Hollow

## Series Identity
A cozy magical sports-and-harvest adventure series about the little purple people of Amihan Hollow, whose moon-fed ube gardens help their village glow, heal, and celebrate.

## What Makes The Ube Folk Visually Original
- They are small **pear-shaped purple people** with broad soft torsos, short sturdy legs, and long balancing feet built for springy field games.
- Their ears are shaped like **folded leaf-sails**, tilting outward like little pennants in the wind.
- Their clothing centers on **woven sash-belts, seed-pocket vests, rain-cape ponchos, and fan-shaped harvest hats** made from dried ube leaves and river grass.
- They carry **loop baskets** on their backs instead of backpacks, and many wear ankle ribbons that flutter when they run.
- Their village architecture uses **terraced yam mounds, bamboo footbridges, hanging lantern gourds, and wind-drying racks**, not mushroom houses or European elf cottages.

## Tone
- playful
- cozy
- magical
- sporty
- community-centered
- emotionally warm
- gently educational

## Core World
- **Amihan Hollow** — a breezy valley of terraced ube gardens, stream-polished courts, and moonwater irrigation channels.
- **Taro Village** — friendly rivals across the river, known for fast feet, teamwork, and clever taro-field drills.
- **Glow Ube** — special purple yam roots that can hold moonlight, warm cold soil, brighten lanterns, and help tired gardens recover when tended respectfully.

## Core Cast
- **Mina** — quick-thinking captain of the Hollow relay team; wears a fan-leaf visor and keeps a notebook of garden observations.
- **Toto** — strongest digger on the team; broad smile, seed-pocket vest, and a habit of cheering too early.
- **Ilya** — careful sprinter and best passer in the village games; wears ankle ribbons that help keep rhythm.
- **Bembem** — funny inventor of sports tools and harvest gadgets; always has twine, clips, and an extra spoon.
- **Apo Sela** — elder grower who teaches that ube is food, memory, and magic only when shared with gratitude.
- **The Taro Village Striders** — neighboring friends and rivals who bring speed, grace, and good sportsmanship to every match.

## Series Rules
- Ube is magical, but the magic comes alive only through care, patience, and community.
- The Ube Folk protect their harvest without cruelty; cleverness and teamwork solve problems.
- Sports should feel joyful and character-building, never mean.
- Educational details about ube should feel woven into action, meals, games, or gardening.
- Outsiders may cause trouble, but the emotional center remains safe, warm, and kid-friendly.

## Visual Direction
- Lush ube terraces with purple leaves, violet soil tones, moonwater channels, and river-stone game courts.
- Warm Filipino-inspired food and planting culture touches: woven baskets, community cooking, shared harvest tables, respectful references to ube as a beloved purple yam.
- Natural representation across both villages in clothing, features, hair textures/styles, and family structures.
- Cozy palette: ube violet, leaf green, moon-silver, yam cream, river teal, clay brown, lantern gold.
